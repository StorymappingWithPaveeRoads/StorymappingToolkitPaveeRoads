1 Introduction - What is Storymapping?

Stories can be told in many different ways, and having the ability to show where our stories take place can add and important and powerful dimension to stories. While all stories indicate some kind of place and location, storymaps allow for explicit showing of the travelling through places in linear narratives.

In other words, storymaps allow us to focus on the ‘where’ of our stories.

The term Storymap, sometimes written as StoryMap, as a term has recently become a shorthand description for online, interactive, linear storytelling built around a map or multiple maps. The maps change as you read them, and often scrolling through the map progresses the story and changes where the map focuses, what is on the map, or introduces other different types of media, including pictures, videos, audio-recordings and more.

In this storymapping toolkit, you will find all the steps to create and design your first storymap. This toolkit includes a guide to all of the technical steps to locating the places, adding descriptions, text and images and creating a fully designed spatial narrative. In addition, this toolkit includes a guide and discussion of how to use storymaps to uniquely tell spatial stories, and how you can design your storymap to showcase places the usefulness of the storymap format.

The guide requires only that you have a browser and a Google account. Some familiarity with Google Sheets or spreadsheets will be helpful, but the guide will also include instructions on all aspects of the storymapping process.

Also accompanied with this written guide are videos of each step of the storymapping process. These can be a helpful addendum or primary resource for carrying out the steps.
